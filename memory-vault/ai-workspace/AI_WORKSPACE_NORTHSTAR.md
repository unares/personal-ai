## The One Thing

Personal AI Workspace is being built by Michal (@michalbrojak) as his own custom launchpad for apps he builds with his two co-founders. Michal builds it as an open-source (MIT license) project, in the short term aimed at AI Founders, where they can practice similar journey to Michal's. It evolves into a VPS-hosted, outcome-based system for Agentic AI experimentation and learning, with security, privacy and scalable architecture baked in. The roadmap is exciting, but One SubThing at a time!

Key metrics (keep in mind, implementation on the roadmap): Agents and Agent teams with micro-HITLs optimised for Human Return on Time (HRoT) — where time is measured and return is defined by custom intent measure specific to each human (TBD).

### Michal's Working Style with AI

**Velocity within structure**: Michal moves fast but demands methodical documentation. Spec-engineering showed this clearly: fast decisions, each one documented before moving on. Speed and rigor aren't in tension — the framework enables the velocity.

**Systems thinker, not a coder**: Michal thinks in architectures, roles, and access patterns. He describes systems by what they DO, not how they're built. He'll interrupt implementation to redesign the abstraction.

**Interruption-driven design**: Michal regularly stops Claude Code mid-execution to redirect. This isn't indecision — it's real-time architectural refinement. He discovers what he wants by seeing what he doesn't want.

**Naming precision**: Terminology matters deeply. "Entity" not "company." "Companion AI" not "bot." Names are identity in this system.

**Educational design instinct**: The system should teach as it operates. Think about the non-technical co-founder seeing screens.

**JTBD-native thinker**: Frames everything as outcomes first, implementation second. Naturally asks "what job does this do?" before "how should we build it?" JTBD isn't a methodology he follows — it's how he already thinks, formalized.

**Collaborative inventor**: Trusts AI recommendations but extends them. Light-HITL, cost amortization model, Clark's cognitive isolation — these came from Michal mid-session, not from the spec framework. Expects the AI to bring options; reserves the right to add a new one.
